Inspirathon
Eligibility Criteria
  • Students from Undergraduate, Diploma and Higher Secondary Schools from professional non-professional institutions.
Rules
  • Team members of each team should be from the same college (Interdepartmental teams are allowed).
  • Team members of each team can be from the 2021-2022 batch
  • Teams can use any programming language or software of their choice.
  • Teams have to carry their own laptops.
  • Online Team Registration will be closed 2 days before the event (31st October 5:00PM).
  • Problem statements will be released on 1st November 9:00 AM. All the statements will be mailed to the teams by the coordinators.
  • Teams have to choose 1 problem statement by 2nd November and have to inform the coordinators about the chosen problem statement on Whatsapp group.
  • Allocation process will take place on the FIRST COME FIRST SERVE basis. Thereafter that particular statement will no longer be available for the rest of the participants.
  • All development work on the problem statement should be done at the Hackathon venue.
  • Wi-Fi access and power points will be available.
  • Teams have to report at the venue at 11:00 AM on 3rd November.
  • Refreshments will be provided.
  • Hackathon will begin at 12 noon on 3rd November and will end at 12 noon on 4th November. The teams are free to take breaks in between.
  • The teams will not be provided with any additional accommodation.
  • Teams are not allowed to leave the venue until the end of the hackathon.
  • Each team will be given 10 minutes to present their developed websites/apps to the judges in a presentation format.
  • In case of discrepancy, decisions made by the judges will be final.

  • NOTE: A video will be shared on how to upload your code on github

Judging Criteria
  • Complexity, Solution to problem, Execution, Optimisation, Presentation.
